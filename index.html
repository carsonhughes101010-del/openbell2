Draft Strategy Guide
The 5 Rules of Elite Drafters
1. Fade the Consensus
The most-drafted stocks carry the most downside surprise risk. Check the Retail Sentiment tab — if everyone owns NVDA, your edge is finding the next NVDA, not owning it.
2. One High-Beta, Four Steady
Your moonshot can win the week. But 4 steady performers protect your floor. Don't draft 5 lottery tickets — one blow-up tanks the portfolio.
3. Sector Rotation is the Game
The weekly winner is usually a sector story. Identify which sector has a catalyst — earnings, macro data, geopolitical event — and overweight that theme.
4. Earnings Week = Edge Week
Stocks with earnings during the draft week have high variance. A beat can move a stock 10%+ in one day. Know the earnings calendar before you draft.
5. Beat the S&P, Not Just the Room
Your real benchmark is SPY. If you go +2% but the market went +3%, you underperformed. Think like a pool manager — aim to beat the index.
Portfolio Frameworks
MOMENTUM
3 trending sector leaders + 2 index ETFs. Ride what's already working.
CONTRARIAN
2 beaten-down names with catalyst + 3 defensive plays. Buy fear.
EARNINGS PLAY
2–3 stocks reporting earnings this week. High variance, high reward.
MACRO HEDGE
Mix commodities (GLD/USO) with tech. Hedge against uncertainty.
Know the language
Investor Glossary
EPS
Earnings Per Share — company profit divided by shares outstanding. The primary earnings metric.
Beat / Miss
When actual EPS is above (beat) or below (miss) analyst estimates. Beats drive stocks up, misses sink them.
Beta
How much a stock moves relative to the market. Beta >1 means more volatile than SPY. TSLA beta ~2.0.
Sector Rotation
When investors move money between sectors — e.g., from tech into energy — usually driven by macro shifts.
P/E Ratio
Price-to-Earnings — how much investors pay per $1 of profit. High P/E = expensive. Low P/E = cheap or troubled.
YTD
Year to Date — return since January 1st of the current calendar year.
Short Interest
% of shares being bet against (shorted). High short interest can lead to a short squeeze when the stock rises.
Guidance
Management's forecast for future earnings/revenue. Often more important to the stock price than the current quarter.
Catalyst
A specific event that can move a stock — earnings, FDA approval, contract win, Fed announcement, etc.
VIX
The "Fear Index." Measures expected volatility. VIX above 20 = nervous market. Above 30 = fear. Above 40 = panic.
Support / Resistance
Price levels where stocks tend to stop falling (support) or struggle to break above (resistance).
Market Cap
Total market value = share price × shares outstanding. Large cap: >$10B. Mid cap: $2–10B. Small cap: <$2B.
Macro
Macroeconomics — big-picture factors: Fed rates, inflation, jobs data, GDP. Drives all sectors simultaneously.

Openbell Rules
✕
🏆
How a Winner Is Determined
The Rule
The player whose 5-stock portfolio has the highest average % return since Monday's open at Friday's 4:00 PM ET close is declared the winner. Each pick carries equal 20% weight — there are no multipliers, no power plays, and no luck involved. Pure stock-picking skill.
✅ Clear Winner
If one player's average return is strictly higher than all others, they win the full payout for their position (1st, 2nd, or 3rd).
🤝 Tie Rule
If two or more players share the exact same average return, they equally split the purse for their tied position. Example: two players tie for 1st in a Winner-Takes-All fund — they each receive 47.5% of the pool.
1
How Returns Are Calculated
Equal-Weight Portfolio
Each of your 5 picks receives an equal 20% allocation of your hypothetical portfolio. Your total return is the simple average of each stock's percentage change from Monday's opening price to Friday's close.
Example
NVDA
+4.2%
× 20%
TSLA
−1.8%
× 20%
META
+2.1%
× 20%
If your 5 picks return +4.2%, −1.8%, +2.1%, +0.6%, +3.2% — your portfolio return is (4.2 − 1.8 + 2.1 + 0.6 + 3.2) ÷ 5 = +1.66%. Every position carries the same weight — no single pick dominates.
Returns are measured from each stock's Monday opening price to Friday's closing price. Prices are sourced from Yahoo Finance in real time during market hours (9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET).
2
Funds & Drafting
A Pool is a weekly competition between you and your group. One person creates it, sets the entry fee, and shares an access code. Everyone else joins with that code and submits their 5 picks before Monday's market open.
Draft Window
Opens Saturday after the previous week closes. Submissions accepted until Monday at 9:30 AM market open.
Live Week
Monday through Friday, the standings board updates in real time as markets move. Winner is determined at Friday's 4:00 PM close.
